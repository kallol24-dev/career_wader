Normative Questions for Career Counseling

In career Counselling normally the two major ones to be posed in the form of questions/ offerings are: 1) Questions testing interests/ aptitudes/skills/ personality and behavioral and motivation traits to explore the self and 2) Offerings/ details presented to the candidate taking the tests, the options of study and employment related to their future career’s pathways/ careers/jobs. These are supposed to be the normative parts of career counselling. But there are lot of non- normative questions have also to be posited, and answers elicited, if the counselling is to be complete and correct conclusions and suggestions on careers are offered to them especially in the Indian context. As no such questionnaires are normally available, it’s better that at Career Wader, we must keep them as sets of questionnaires in post- test assessment sessions. I have posed them with the right prompts to get these questionnaires elicited. Some of these are applicable to only transitioning professionals and some others to even school goers too. Please keep these with any additions made to them at your end just like the pre-assessment questionnaires.

Here's a suggested battery of questions for each of the 10 non-normative influences:

Career Wader: Post-Test Assessment – Non-Normative Influences Questionnaire

To be used during one-on-one sessions after aptitude and personality assessments. This helps to contextualize results and personalize career recommendations.

Set 1: Influence of Family, Community, and Significant Others
Use for: All candidates, especially students.
  • Who are the people that influence your career choices the most? (e.g., parents, friends, teachers)
  • How do their opinions impact your decisions about education or work?
  • Have you felt pressured to pursue a particular path? How did you handle it?
  • Share a time when you had to balance your career interests with others' expectations.

Counselor Tip: Look for conflicts between internal desires and external expectations.

Set 2: Gender Sensitivities
Use for: All candidates, especially adolescents and women returners.
  • Do you think certain careers are more suitable for your gender? Why?
  • Have you faced gender-based bias or stereotypes while choosing a career? How did you respond?
  • How do you think gender has impacted your career choices or opportunities?
  • Are there careers you think are less accessible to your gender? Why?
Set 3: Rural to Urban Transition
Use for: Migrant students, rural-origin professionals.
  • Have you moved from a rural to urban setting for study/work? What challenges did you face?
  • How does your background (rural/urban) shape your career goals?
  • What are the key differences you've seen in opportunities between rural and urban settings?
  • How do you plan to adapt to the urban/rural job market?
Set 4: Labor Market Awareness vs. Multi-potentiality
Use for: College students, mid-career professionals.
  • How do you stay updated about current job market trends?
  • Do you think you’re capable of multiple career paths? Why?
  • Name 2–3 careers you're interested in. How do they match current demand?
  • How will you balance your passion with employability?

Optional Add-on: Counselor may share 1–2 labor market reports or skill projections.

Set 5: Career Decision-Making Capacity
Use for: High school and college students.
  • How confident are you in making career decisions?
  • What factors guide your career decisions? (e.g., income, interest, family)
  • Describe a tough career decision you made and how you approached it.
  • How can you improve your decision-making further?
Set 6: Literacy Skills
Use for: All segments.
  • What skills are essential for your dream career?
  • How do you plan to build or improve these skills?
  • Are there any skills gap you need to work on?
  • How do you stay informed about new skills in your field?

Optional Tool: Provide checklist of 21st-century or sector-specific skills.

Set 7: Transitioning from Unskilled to Skilled
Use for: School dropouts, low-income adults, vocational learners.
  • Do you feel capable of transitioning into a skilled job? Why/why not?
  • What training do you think you need?
  • Which industry or job roles interest you for upskilling?
  • How will you go about acquiring skills (courses, apprenticeships, etc.)?
Set 8: Special Needs/Health Constraints
Use for: Candidates with health issues, disabilities, neurodivergence.
  • Do you have any special needs that may impact career decisions?
  • How do you plan to accommodate these needs?
  • What support or resources would you like access to?
  • How comfortable are you in communicating your needs to potential employers/educators?

Counselor Caution: Ensure sensitivity and informed suggestions (e.g., disability-friendly employers).

Set 9: Social Class and Economic Barriers
Use for: All, especially first-generation learners.
  • How does your socio-economic background affect your career choices?
  • Have you faced financial or social hurdles in pursuing a career goal?
  • Are there careers that feel “out of reach” due to finances or class? Which?
  • What strategies or support would help you overcome these challenges?
Set 10: Career Beliefs and Worldview
Use for: All candidates
  • What do you believe makes a career “successful”?
  • Are there any beliefs that may be limiting your career choices? (e.g., “only engineers succeed”)
  • How will you challenge or rethink these limiting beliefs?
  • Have any mentors or role models influenced your career thinking?
